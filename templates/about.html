üìä How This Calculator Works

1. PDF Extraction

The system automatically detects your broker (Interactive Brokers or Saxo Bank) and extracts all stock transactions from your PDF statements. It identifies:

  • Transaction dates
  • Stock symbols
  • Buy/Sell type
  • Number of shares
  • Transaction amounts
  • Currencies

2. Transaction Grouping

Same-side transactions are grouped: Multiple purchases or sales of the same stock on the same day are combined into a single transaction.

Day trades remain separate: If you buy AND sell the same stock on the same day, both transactions are kept separate because Belgian law taxes both sides of a day trade.

3. Currency Conversion

All foreign currency amounts are converted to EUR using official European Central Bank (ECB) reference rates for each transaction date.

This applies to both Interactive Brokers and Saxo Bank statements. The calculator automatically detects the transaction currency and applies the appropriate ECB exchange rate for that date.

üì• How to Download Interactive Brokers Statements

To get the correct statement format for this calculator:

  1. Log in to your Interactive Brokers account
  2. Navigate to Performance & Reports ‚Üí Statements
  3. Select Activity Statement (can be for a full month or custom period)
  4. Choose PDF format and download

Note: Activity Statements work best. Other statements that include "Trades" sections may also work.

üì• How to Download Saxo Bank Statements

To get the correct statement format for this calculator:

  1. Log in to your Saxo Bank account
  2. Navigate to Rekeningoverzichten (Account Statements)
  3. Click on Overzichten Downloaden (Download Statements)
  4. Select Transactie- en saldorapport (Transaction and Balance Report)
  5. Choose your date range and download as PDF

Note: Make sure to select "Transactie- en saldorapport" and not other report types, as the calculator is specifically designed for this format.
